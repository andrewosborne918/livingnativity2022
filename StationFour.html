Station 4: The Magnificat and Joseph’s Dream

Click "play" for audio.

Moving forward, there is a scene of dual perspectives. On the one side, you see Mary. A woman who once was so confused. Once so struck with fear. But now rejoicing so greatly. Bursting with joy she sang, “My soul magnifies the Lord, and my spirit rejoices in God my Savior, for he has looked on the humble estate of his servant.”

On the other side, you see Joseph. An ordinary man much like Mary a ordinary woman. He was engaged to Mary, and yet somehow she was pregnant. How could this be! Confusion, distress, even thoughts of separation plagued his mind. When suddenly, a messenger of God appeared to Him saying, Joseph, son of David, do not fear to take Mary as your wife, for that which is conceived in her is from the Holy Spirit.” of God appeared to Him saying, “Joseph, son of David, do not fear to take Mary as your wife, for that which is conceived in her is from the Holy Spirit.”
